                        📊 2. Calculus Equations
                    


                    
                    

                        Total Live Body Weight f(x)
                        

                            f(x) = -0.021x³ + 1.85x² + 5.2x + 42
                        

                        

                            Yields the average cumulative mass (grams) on day x. The constant 42 represents standard Malaysian day-old chick hatch weight.
                        

                    


                    
                    

                        Daily Growth Velocity f'(x)
                        

                            f'(x) = -0.063x² + 3.70x + 5.2
                        

                        

                            The derivative calculated using the Power Rule. Yields the instantaneous growth speed in grams/day on day x.
                        

                    

                


                
                

                    

                        🧬 Malaysian Context
                    

                    

                        In Malaysia, ambient conditions often climb past the thermal comfort zone of modern broiler lines ($18\text{--}24^\circ\text{C}$). When raised in open-sided ventilation systems, high humidity levels limit evaporative cooling. 
                    

                    

                        This metabolic stress shifts the inflection point—the day growth velocity peaks—forcing earlier, less profitable plateaus. Finding this maximum using calculus helps farm managers schedule feed phase transitions accurately.
